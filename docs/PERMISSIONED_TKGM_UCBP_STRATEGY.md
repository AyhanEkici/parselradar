# Permissioned TKGM UCBP Strategy

## 1) Purpose
Define a legal-safe, permissioned strategy for TKGM/UCBP-related product direction without introducing unauthorized automation.

## 2) Legal-safe positioning
ParselRadar remains guidance-first and evidence-organization-first. Official-data automation requires explicit legal authority and approved technical access paths.

## 3) What is allowed now
- manual evidence upload
- user-provided screenshots/documents
- public metadata/source guidance
- permissioned/manual review workflows

## 4) What is forbidden
- scraping
- e-Devlet credential collection
- e-Devlet automation
- CAPTCHA bypass
- unauthorized TKGM/MEGSIS automation
- fake official verification claims

## 5) UCBP/TUCBS access strategy
- keep connector posture governed and non-active unless approval path is complete
- use manual evidence + guidance for current controlled beta
- move to technical activation only after legal + operational gates pass

## 6) Individual vs company/MERSIS access consideration
- individual portal visibility does not equal platform connector authority
- company/MERSIS or institutional requirements must be treated as governance blockers until satisfied

## 7) Evidence policy
Evidence taxonomy and wording must continue to separate:
- user-provided official evidence
- public-source guidance
- verified connector-backed evidence
- unverified/missing/manual-required states

## 8) Connector activation governance
Activation requires legal approval, endpoint/protocol verification, auditable decisioning, and rollback readiness. Convenience access is not sufficient.

## 9) Future permissioned integration criteria
All required before activation:
- legal right to use data for platform scope
- approved access mode (individual/company/institution as required)
- endpoint/service verification (WMS/WFS/WMTS or approved equivalent)
- credential and security review
- compliance and audit sign-off

## 10) Manual official evidence workflow
Manual portal checks and uploads remain valid controlled-beta workflow with explicit trust boundaries and no automation claims.

## 11) Product wording boundaries
Product copy must continue to state:
- guidance only
- official source must be checked manually when applicable
- not official property verification unless explicitly approved and verified

## 12) Risk register
- Legal risk: high if unauthorized automation is attempted
- Security risk: high if credential/session handling is expanded improperly
- Trust risk: high if wording over-claims official certainty
- Operational risk: medium for future integration complexity

## 13) Controlled beta recommendation
Proceed with permissioned/manual route only. Keep automated official-data claims disabled.

## 14) Public launch requirements
If official-data claims are desired, public launch requires approved permissioned integration pathway and successful compliance verification.

## 15) Final recommendation
- Permissioned only.
- No scraping.
- No e-Devlet automation.
- No official verification claim until a legally approved source path is implemented and verified.
- Manual evidence remains valid for controlled beta.
